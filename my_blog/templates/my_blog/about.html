Who the Python Novice writer is:


Hey there! Thanks for continuing reading, let’s make this rather quick, you’ll read more in my posts as they come out. I am someone that is not good at following through with something that I am not passionate about. So, although I consider myself to be smart, I am not very disciplined with something that I do not enjoy. With that said, here is a little bit about who I am and a little bit about my professional path.

Since little I’ve always wanted to make a difference and build something significant, something that would make an impact on someone’s life. I started studying chemistry in high school and fell in love with it. I was good at it even though I was just above average in math and physics, I still loved chemistry and I’d always give more from myself when it came to studying, which dedicating time to studying has always been my weakness. However, I dropped out because I needed money, and joined a call center, which on the first day I knew was something I never wanted to do again. Fast forward a year or two, with the help of a great friend, my ability to learn systems and processes rather quickly, as well as, my critical and analytical mindset got me a job as an Accounts Receivable Associate in a large company with good pay. I never thought of myself as a person in a cubicle but I actually enjoyed it, time would fly by and I end up staying after hours without noticing.

Now, most recently, coding is the thing giving me that feeling, the feeling that time flies. I know I may have started for what I think are the wrong reasons, but I definitely stayed for all the right ones. I can stay for hours trying my best to solve a problem in codewars or trying to get a form/button to work, and yet I still feel whole. I love reading the documentation to learn new processes and I always look forward to what's ahead, and in programming the possibilities for learning and creating are endless.
